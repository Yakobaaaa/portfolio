01 — Who

No paper. Just receipts.

I started messing with electronics at 12. Burned out a few resistors, fried one Arduino, and somewhere along the way it stopped being a hobby. I'm Yakob — a software and hardware engineer who doesn't have a degree and isn't waiting for one.

I work both sides of the stack. Firmware on bare-metal MCUs — Arduino, ESP32, PIC, and whatever dev board is on my desk that week. React frontends. Node.js services. Python scripts. C, C++, C#, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, a bit of assembly when the chip asks for it. PCB layouts in KiCad. To me it's all the same thing — different syntax, same logic. If a system can be built, I want to try.

I don't chase shipping numbers. I build when someone asks me to. Right now I work locally out of Ethiopia — delivering projects on request, not selling products to a marketplace. What I really do is give life to machines. That's the part I'm here for — the moment a thing just works. When the LED blinks. When the query is 200x faster. When the drone hovers exactly where it's supposed to.

— Yakob
